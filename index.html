CVS Self-Checkout:
Is It Really Worth It?

New Skills Acquired:
HTML & CSS, Bootstrap, Developing User Personas, Storyboarding

In this project, I investigated the often-overlooked consumer experience of the CVS Self-Checkout Machine. Based on my own experiences with CVS self-checkout, I formulated interview questions off of which I could determine some "standard" user experience types. I was able to classify my findings into two main categories: "Anxious" and "Efficient" users. After developing personas on these two archetypes, I storyboarded a common experience of the "Anxious". Finally, I reported my findings into my first website using HTML & CSS!

The North Dakota Rodeo Association:
A Needed Redesign

New Skills Acquired:
Usability, Accessibility, Prototyping and Wireframing in Figma, Responsiveness

In this project, I investigated the usability and accessibility issues of the official website of the North Dakota Rodeo Association. With the website information and flaws in mind, I developed lo-fi and high-fi wireframe prototypes for what an improved version of the site might look like. Finally, I utilized my growing knowledge of HTML, CSS, and Bootstrap to reconfigure and redesign the NDRA website. In doing so, I also explored making the site responsive to different device screen sizes. This was a major flaw of the original website, so I changed the overall framework of the website for laptop, tablet, and phone screen sizes.

An Iterative Redesign for Fetii

New Skills Acquired:
Developing a Comprehensive Prototype, User Testing

In this project, I worked with a group of three other UI/UX students to develop a comprehensive prototype for Fetii by combining many different sketch ideas and collaboratively creating a High Fidelity Wireframe in Figma. After our prototype was ready, we participated in a group critique where we collected feedback on our design and revised our prototype based on the feedback we received. Finally, we performed a user testing experiment for our prototype by devising tasks for three random participants to see how well they were able to interact with our prototype.

The IKEA Index

New Skills Acquired:
React, JavaScript, User Interaction

In this project, I developed an interactive application using React and JavaScript that allows for a simplified browsing experience of the IKEA catalog. IKEA's website, while more comprehensive, does not allow for quick and easy access to all pieces of furniture in a given category -- be it room, style, or color. Users can also save their possible purchase for future reference and research on the IKEA main website.
